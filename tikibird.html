Custom Tiki Bird Animatronic

Mechanical, Electrical, Sofware, and Creative Design

With theme park animatronics tracing much of their roots back to the 1963 Enchanted Tiki Room, this was a project that I had always wanted to make.

To start, I hand-sculpted the head, beak, and tongue from an oil-based clay, created silicone jacket molds, and cast the parts in a durable and paint-friendly polyurethane resin. The 2-DOF motion base for the neck and beak actuator assembly were all modeled in Fusion 360 and 3D printed. The most challenging part of this project was creating the internal beak assembly to fit within the initial sculpt and maintain the proper jaw pivot point, since the sculpt was done prior to the internal mechanism.

When it came to programming, I wanted fluid animation as well as the ability to visualize the figure while creating shows. The solution was inverse-kinematics. To live-program the figure from Blender, I was able to stream desired actuator positions via serial communication to the control board. Once I was happy with a program, I developed a pipeline to export the finished animations and audio to an SD card, which the controller was able to playback without being tethered to a computer.

  • Date: August 2024
  • Skills: Sculping, Moldmaking/Casting, Design
  • Tools: Fusion 360, Blender, C++, Python
